8. Application — radioactive decay.

A radioactive isotope has a half-life of 4 years. A sample contains 80 grams initially.

a. Write an equation A(t) for the amount of the isotope remaining (in grams) after t years.
b. How much of the sample remains after 12 years?
c. After how many years will only 5 grams remain? Solve exactly.
d. Hugo claims the sample will eventually decay to 0 grams. Is he correct? Justify your answer mathematically.
e. Find the exact yearly decay rate of this isotope. (Express your answer as a constant — no decimals.)

9. Compound interest application.

Hugo invests $4,000 in an account that pays 6% annual interest, compounded quarterly.

a. Write an equation A(t) for the value of the account after t years.
b. How much will the account be worth after 5 years? Leave your answer in exact form.
c. After how many years will the investment double (reach $8,000)? Solve exactly using logs.
d. Hugo's friend Frank wants to also reach $8,000 in 5 years, starting from $4,000, but in an account compounded annually. What annual interest rate r would Frank need? Solve exactly.
